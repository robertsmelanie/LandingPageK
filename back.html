Check out today's selection and step back in time with this authentic World War II-era flat-top house from Oak Ridge, Tennessee. Designed by the renowned architectural firm Skidmore, Owings & Merrill, these prefabricated homes were integral to the Manhattan Project, providing efficient housing for thousands of workers in the secret city of Oak Ridge. Constructed with Cemesto panels—a combination of cement and asbestos—these structures exemplify the ingenuity and rapid development of wartime America. Owning this piece offers a tangible connection to a pivotal moment in history, making it a must-have for collectors and history enthusiasts alike.
